(Photo: Courtesy of Sartor)

LONDON: The Russian government has taken the necessary step toward removing Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov from its high-level advisory group.

The new council will be held on September 15 – which is just two weeks away from the anniversary of the annexation of Crimea by Russian-backed separatists in eastern Ukraine .

After Lavrov and Russian President Vladimir Putin visited Ukraine and the Baltic states in the spring, the two countries shared an initial goal of building up a "front line in Ukraine, and on the world stage.

Russia and the US have been at loggerheads over Crimea.
